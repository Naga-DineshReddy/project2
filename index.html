Historical Sites of Kadapa

Explore the rich historical heritage of Kadapa through its ancient forts, temples, and monuments.

Fort & Canyon

Gandikota Fort

Built in the 13th century on the banks of the Pennar River, Gandikota Fort is known as the "Grand Canyon of India." The fort was constructed by Kakatiya rulers and later controlled by Vijayanagara Empire, Qutb Shahi rulers, and the British.

The fort features 5km long granite walls, Madhavaraya Temple, Ranganatha Swamy Temple, granaries, mosques, and water storage systems. It's a protected monument by ASI and a major tourist attraction.

Ancient Fort

Siddavatam Fort

Located on the banks of the Pennar River, this 14th century fort was built by Kakatiya rulers. It played a vital role in controlling trade and military routes across the region.

The fort features massive stone walls, bastions, gateways, temples, mosques, and step wells. It came under Vijayanagara Empire, Qutb Shahi dynasty, and Nawabs of Kadapa, showcasing a mix of architectural styles.

Temple

Vontimitta Kodanda Rama Swamy Temple

Constructed in the 16th century under Vijayanagara Empire patronage, this temple is dedicated to Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana. It gained importance during Krishnadevaraya's reign.

Built in classical Vijayanagara style with grand stone pillars, open mandapas, and detailed carvings. Unlike many temples, it doesn't have a traditional gopuram. Associated with saints Bhadrachala Ramadasu and Annamacharya.

Sufi Shrine

Ameen Peer Dargah

One of the oldest and most respected Sufi shrines in Rayalaseema, dedicated to Hazrat Shaikh Ameen-ud-Din (17th century). He spread messages of peace, unity, and brotherhood.

Gained importance during Qutb Shahi rule and became a center for spiritual learning and social unity. A symbol of religious tolerance and communal harmony, attracting thousands during Urs festival.

Ancient Temples

Pushpagiri Temples

An ancient group of Hindu temples from the 7th century on the banks of Pennar River, dedicated mainly to Lord Shiva. Received patronage from Pallava, Chola, and Vijayanagara rulers.

An important center of Shaivism with early Dravidian-style architecture, stone structures, carved pillars, and inscriptions. Though many structures are in ruins, they remain significant archaeological sites.

View on Map
Sacred Temple

Tholi Gadapa (Devuni Kadapa)

An ancient temple dedicated to Lord Venkateswara, considered the first threshold before visiting Tirumala. Dates back to ancient times and associated with Vaishnavism.

Gained prominence during Vijayanagara Empire. Built in traditional South Indian (Dravidian) style. Devotees visit this temple first before proceeding to Tirumala Tirupati.
